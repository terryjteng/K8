From Micro-Game to Funded Studio

K.8 Studios is using a deliberately small, shippable title – Hotdog Hustler – to fund the formation of the studio, validate our workflows, and build a polished demo for Last Light.

Our Strategy

The Game Plan

We’re a studio built on shipping. Our path from first concept to funded studio is deliberately narrow and milestone-driven: prove we can ship, use revenue to stand up the studio, then build a publisher-ready demo for Last Light.

1

Build & Ship Hotdog Hustler

First priority. Complete development, polish, and launch to generate initial revenue and prove we can ship quality titles on time.

2

Fund Studio Setup

Use Hotdog Hustler revenue to establish K.8 Studios LLC, handle legal and accounting, and cover lightweight operational costs.

3

Develop the Last Light Demo

With core infrastructure in place, build a tight, vertical slice of Last Light that shows our full capabilities.

4

Secure Publishing / Investment

Leverage a playable demo and proven shipping track record to attract publisher or micro-angel funding for full production.

Funding Plan: Hotdog Hustler → Last Light Demo

We’ve modeled revenue from Hotdog Hustler at both a standard premium price and a $1 micro-price, then mapped that to concrete milestones: studio setup, demo production, and investor pitches.

Phase 1 — Revenue via Hotdog Hustler

Revenue Phase

Ship a cozy 2D hotdog cart simulator on Steam/itch.io in ~2 months, using a lean revenue-share team and micro-scope design to ensure completion and polish.

  • $4.99 base price with targeted $1 sales events
  • Steam & itch.io distribution
  • 50% of net revenue dedicated to studio fund

Phase 2 — Establish K.8 Studios LLC

Operations Phase

Use early revenue to formally create the California LLC, open business accounts, and set up core tools and legal infrastructure.

  • LLC registration, EIN, operating agreement
  • Accounting & banking setup
  • Website & studio communications (already in place)

Phase 3 — Build the Last Light Demo

Production Phase

Invest in a tight vertical slice of Last Light – one biome, core loop, and a playable demo designed specifically for publisher/investor review.

  • Unity dev: $2.5K–$6K
  • Art: $1K–$3K
  • Sound: $300–$1K
  • QA: $200–$600

Funding Scenarios

Self-Funded + Hustler Revenue

Use Hotdog Hustler revenue plus small personal investment to fund both studio setup and demo build. Requires ~1,500–4,000 units at $4.99 or ~7,000–22,000 at $1.00.

Hybrid / Investor-Backed (Recommended)

Let Hustler fund studio formation, build 10–20% of the demo, then leverage traction and prototype into micro-angel/publisher funding ($20K–$75K range) for a publisher-quality demo and marketing runway.

Execution Roadmap

We’ve already translated vision into concrete, timeboxed execution – with sprints, roles, and deliverables defined from pre-production to release.

Pre-Production

~1 Week

  • Team onboarding & ClickUp setup
  • GitHub repo creation & architecture outline
  • Art tests & finalized topping list

Sprints 1–3: Core Loop

Weeks 1–3

  • Input, cart state machine, and core interactions
  • Topping system & basic UI wired
  • Customer spawning, orders, timers, and end-of-day summary
  • By Sprint 3: full loop playable, no new features added

Sprints 4–6: Content, Polish, Release

Weeks 4–6 (+ buffer)

  • Content completion & animation integration
  • Polish/QA with multiple playtest rounds
  • Steam/itch.io build pipeline and store assets
