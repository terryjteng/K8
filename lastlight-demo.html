Last Light · Demo Build

Demo Plan & Development

Last Light is a 2D top-down survival shooter where players defend a fragile home base from incoming zombie waves while exploring the surrounding area to gather wood, stone, and metal. These resources are used to repair the base and craft automated turrets — but all turrets and the player share one global ammo pool, creating tension and meaningful strategic decisions.

Scope

Development Scope & Systems

The demo showcases core systems with planned expansion for the full game:

  • Player Controller & Combat: Responsive movement (4/8 direction), melee weapon (machete), ranged combat with bullet system, dash/roll mechanics, and polished hit-stop feedback
  • Enemy AI & Wave System: Zombie state machine (idle/wander, detect, chase, attack), pathfinding, configurable wave spawner with difficulty curves, and telegraphed spawn warnings
  • Resource & Crafting: Three-tier resource nodes (wood, stone, metal), inventory tracking, respawn cooldowns, and turret crafting recipes
  • Base Defense: Base HP system, repair mechanics, turret placement with valid/invalid zones, auto-targeting AI, and the signature shared ammo pool
  • Art & Polish: Complete character animations, environment tilesets, VFX (muzzle flashes, hits, harvest effects), UI/HUD, and atmospheric sound design

What's Included in the Demo

  • Single playable map with base, resource zones, and danger areas
  • Complete combat system (melee + ranged)
  • One zombie enemy type with scalable difficulty
  • One turret type with auto-targeting
  • Shared ammo system (signature mechanic)
  • Resource gathering & crafting (wood, stone, metal)
  • Base repair & defense
  • Wave progression (3–5 waves)
  • Full UI/UX with onboarding prompts
  • Sound design & VFX
  • Performance optimization (60 FPS target)

Production

Demo Milestones

The demo is treated as a vertical slice with clear, gated milestones from design through investor-ready build and materials.

  1. 1. Demo Design & GDD Update

    Update the Last Light Game Design Document to clearly define the demo scope, core mechanics to validate, and win/fail conditions.

  2. 2. Core Loop Prototype

    Implement basic day/night cycle, farming, base building, and a simple zombie encounter to validate “build your base, defend at night” as the core loop.

  3. 3. Visual Direction Lock

    Establish pixel-art style, base/farm tiles, zombies, and key UI elements for the demo slice.

  4. 4. Vertical Slice Build (5–10 Minutes)

    Polish a contained map with the small forest, small town, home base, and one complete day/night cycle that showcases farming, building, and defending.

  5. 5. Pitch Materials

    Create an investor-facing package: updated GDD extract, business one-pager, and short gameplay capture to accompany the demo build.

  6. 6. Publisher & Investor Outreach

    Use the demo and materials in targeted outreach as the foundation for full-game funding conversations.

Design Pillars

What Makes Last Light Unique

Genre Fusion Excellence

We've combined beloved genres—survival shooters, base defense, and resource management—to create a unique experience that feels both familiar and fresh. The best of each genre working together in perfect harmony.

Retro Meets Modern

Combining retro-style pixel art and classic gameplay mechanics with the modern zombie survival theme that has captivated players for years. Timeless gameplay with contemporary appeal.

Endless Replayability

Continuous gameplay that allows users to keep building, defending, and exploring the breadth of this new game style. Each run offers new strategic choices and base-building opportunities.

Investment Angle

Why This Demo Works

The demo demonstrates strong market viability and clear expansion potential:

  • Strong moment-to-moment tension
  • Player agency in resource allocation
  • A unique selling feature (shared ammo)
  • A survival loop with clear expansion potential
  • Systems that scale effortlessly into a full game
  • Modern indie viability — small team, reasonable scope

Low cost demo → high ROI potential when expanded.

Target Audience

Players who enjoy:

  • Survival games (Project Zomboid, 7 Days to Die)
  • Base defense (They Are Billions, Kingdom: Two Crowns)
  • Resource-driven tension (Darkwood, Don't Starve)
  • Indie action games with tight loops (Brotato, Vampire Survivors)
