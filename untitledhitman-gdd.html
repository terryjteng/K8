Untitled Open World Hitman

Game Design Document (GDD)

Open World Stealth-Action Sandbox. Creative assassination meets emergent gameplay in expansive environments.

Studio: Kato.8
Build Path: Concept → Prototype → Vertical Slice → Production
Contact: terryjteng@gmail.com

Executive Summary

  • Concept: Open-world stealth sandbox emphasizing player creativity and emergent assassination scenarios.
  • Hook: Every environment becomes a complex puzzle with multiple creative solutions for elimination objectives.
  • Market: Targets fans of Hitman series, immersive sims like Dishonored, and open-world creativity games.
  • Prototype Goals: Core stealth mechanics, AI behavior systems, environmental interaction framework.
  • Vertical Slice Goals: One complete mission area showcasing emergent gameplay possibilities.

Emergent Creativity

Player ingenuity drives gameplay through systemic interactions and multiple solution paths.

Living World Systems

Dynamic AI routines and reactive environments create opportunities for organic storytelling.

Methodical Freedom

Combine precise stealth mechanics with open-world exploration and discovery.

Core Gameplay Systems

Stealth & Detection

  • Line of sight and audio-based detection
  • Dynamic alertness states and investigation
  • Environmental cover and concealment
  • Social stealth and disguise systems

Environmental Interaction

  • Physics-based object manipulation
  • Environmental hazards and traps
  • Hackable electronics and security systems
  • Destructible elements for creative solutions

Dynamic AI Framework

Behavioral Routines

  • Realistic daily schedules and patrol routes
  • Context-sensitive conversations and interactions
  • Adaptive responses to environmental changes
  • Memory and communication systems between NPCs

Detection & Response

  • Multi-layered awareness states (unaware → suspicious → alerted → hostile)
  • Intelligent search patterns and evidence investigation
  • Coordination between security forces
  • Escalating response protocols based on threat level

Agent Arsenal

Stealth Tools

  • Silenced weapons
  • Distraction devices
  • Lock picks and hacking tools
  • Surveillance equipment

Environmental Tools

  • Rope and grappling gear
  • Explosives and charges
  • Electronic disruptors
  • Chemical compounds

Disguises & Social

  • Context-appropriate uniforms
  • Fake identification
  • Voice modulators
  • Social manipulation tools

Environment Design Principles

Systemic Complexity

Every location designed as interconnected systems that players can observe, understand, and manipulate.

  • Multiple entry and exit points
  • Vertical exploration opportunities
  • Hidden passages and maintenance areas

Narrative Integration

Environmental storytelling woven into gameplay mechanics and exploration rewards.

  • Story fragments discovered through investigation
  • Character backgrounds revealed through observation
  • Mission context emerging from world details

Technology Stack

Engine & Platform

  • Engine: Unreal Engine 5
  • Primary Platform: PC (Windows)
  • Secondary: PlayStation, Xbox
  • Target Performance: 60fps at 1080p

Core Systems

  • Advanced AI behavior tree system
  • Dynamic lighting and shadow casting
  • Physics-based interaction framework
  • Modular level streaming architecture

Production Roadmap

Phase 1: Pre-Production (8 weeks)

Technical design, AI prototyping, core mechanics proof-of-concept

Phase 2: Systems Prototype (16 weeks)

Playable stealth mechanics, basic AI, first environment

Phase 3: Vertical Slice (20 weeks)

Polished demo level, advanced AI, social stealth systems

Phase 4: Production Ready (12 months)

Full team expansion, multiple environments, content pipeline

Project Requirements

Core Team Needs

  • Lead Programmer (systems architecture)
  • AI Programmer (behavior systems)
  • Level Designer (systemic environments)
  • 3D Artist (environment creation)
  • Narrative Designer (environmental storytelling)

Development Philosophy

  • Iterative prototyping approach
  • Player creativity drives design decisions
  • Systemic complexity over scripted sequences
  • Emergent storytelling through gameplay

Development Risks & Mitigation

Technical Challenges

  • AI Complexity: Incremental behavior system development
  • Performance: Modular streaming and LOD systems
  • Systemic Interactions: Extensive prototyping and testing

Design Challenges

  • Scope Management: Milestone-driven development gates
  • Player Guidance: Intuitive tutorial and feedback systems
  • Balance: Regular playtesting and iteration cycles
